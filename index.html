— 01 / Story
Our story · 26 years

The first kitchen in Tashkent dedicated to Uzbek national cuisine.

When Caravan opened its doors in 1999, no other restaurant in the city had committed itself entirely to Uzbek tradition. The space — a traditional family house in Yakkasaray — was built around a courtyard, hand-laid Rishtan ceramics, woven carpets and an artistic wrought-iron staircase that guests still photograph today.

More than two decades later, the recipes haven't moved. Plov by ancient method. Hand-pulled lagman. Tandoor samsa. Slow-simmered shorpa. The chefs you taste today trained under the chefs who opened the house.
