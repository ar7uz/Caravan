Our Story

The first national-cuisine restaurant in Tashkent.

Caravan opened its doors in 1999 — at a moment when Uzbek cuisine was rarely served at this standard outside of family homes. The idea was simple: take dishes that travellers along the old Silk Road would have recognised, and serve them in a setting worthy of them.

Twenty-five years later, the courtyard is still full. Hand-pulled lagman, slow-cooked plov, shashlik over open flame, and shurpa from the kazan are prepared the way they always have been. Live music drifts between Uzbek folklore, ethno-fusion jazz and ambient melodies — performed by musicians known well beyond Uzbekistan.

We are part dining room, part museum of Uzbek applied art: traditional carpets, Rishtan ceramics, custom pottery, and rooms named after the crafts that inspired them.

The House

Six rooms. One courtyard.

From a quiet two-top under the trees to a private hall for celebrations — Caravan is built like a traditional Uzbek house, room by room.

The Courtyard

Open-air seating beneath the trees. Cool even in the Tashkent summer — the favourite of long-time regulars.

Rishtan Room

Named after the legendary ceramic tradition of the Fergana Valley. Walls dressed in hand-painted blue and white tile.

The Terrace & VIP

Live music every evening. A separate VIP room and a second floor available for private events and banquets.
